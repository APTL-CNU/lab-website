Prof. Seho Kweon

Assistant Professor

College of Pharmacy · Chonnam National University

Seho Kweon is an assistant Professor at the College of Pharmacy at Chonnam National University in South Korea. With a Ph.D. from Seoul National University and having research experience at Biomedicine Cutting Edge Formulation Technology Center, he possesses extensive expertise. Before joining academia, he contributed significantly to drug development and regulatory processes at Hanmi Pharmaceuticals Co. Ltd. His current research focuses on developing computational pharmaceutics for biologics, peptide delivery systems for metabolic disease, and applying mathematical models to cancer treatment for translational science.

Research

Exploring the frontiers of AI, robotics, and intelligent systems

Active 🤖

Embodied AI & Robotic Manipulation

We develop learning algorithms that enable robots to acquire dexterous manipulation skills from minimal demonstrations. Our approach combines imitation learning, reinforcement learning, and world models to bridge the gap between simulation and real-world deployment. Key challenges include contact-rich manipulation, deformable object handling, and long-horizon task planning.

Robot Learning Sim-to-Real Transfer Dexterous Manipulation Imitation Learning
Active 🧠

Foundation Models for Scientific Discovery

We adapt and fine-tune large foundation models for scientific tasks including drug discovery, protein structure prediction, and materials design. By developing domain-specific pre-training strategies and evaluation benchmarks, we aim to accelerate the pace of scientific breakthroughs using AI as a research partner.

Large Language Models Drug Discovery Protein Folding Scientific ML
Active 👁️

3D Scene Understanding & Generation

Our research addresses the fundamental challenge of 3D visual understanding from sparse observations. We develop novel neural representations for scenes and objects that enable robots and autonomous systems to build accurate world models from monocular video, enabling downstream tasks like navigation, manipulation, and scene editing.

Neural Radiance Fields 3D Gaussian Splatting SLAM Generative Models
Active 🤝

Human-Robot Collaboration

We study how robots and humans can effectively collaborate on shared tasks in unstructured environments. Our research spans intent prediction, shared autonomy, and natural language interfaces for robot control. We conduct user studies to understand human factors and design robot behaviors that feel natural, safe, and trustworthy.

Human-Robot Interaction Shared Autonomy NLP for Robotics User Studies
Active 🌐

Multi-Agent Systems & Emergent Behavior

We investigate how multiple AI agents can coordinate to solve complex tasks that are beyond the reach of any single agent. Using multi-agent reinforcement learning and game-theoretic approaches, we study emergent communication, cooperative and competitive behaviors, and scalable coordination mechanisms for swarm robotics.

Multi-Agent RL Swarm Robotics Game Theory Emergent Communication
Active ⚡

Efficient & Trustworthy AI

As AI systems are deployed in safety-critical applications, efficiency and trustworthiness become paramount. We research model compression, uncertainty quantification, and robustness verification techniques that make deep learning models more reliable, interpretable, and deployable on resource-constrained hardware.

Model Compression Uncertainty Quantification Robustness Interpretability
